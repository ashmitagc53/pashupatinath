UNESCO World Heritage Site

The Pashupatinath Temple, located on the banks of the Bagmati River in Kathmandu, Nepal, is one of the most significant Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is not just a spiritual destination but also an architectural marvel and a cultural hub.

Key Features and Highlights:

  • Religious Significance

    It is one of the holiest Shiva temples in the world. The temple is considered a sacred pilgrimage site for Hindus and attracts devotees from Nepal, India, and other parts of the world. Lord Shiva is worshipped here in the form of a lingam. The main deity, known as Pashupati, represents the protector of all living beings.

  • Festivals

    The temple is especially vibrant during Maha Shivaratri, when thousands of devotees gather to worship and celebrate. Other Hindu festivals, like Teej and Haritalika, are also celebrated with great fervor.

HISTORY

The Pashupatinath Temple, dating back to around 400 CE, is a sacred Hindu site in Kathmandu, Nepal, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Steeped in mythology and history, it features iconic pagoda-style architecture and remains a UNESCO World Heritage Site, symbolizing Nepal’s rich cultural and spiritual heritage.
